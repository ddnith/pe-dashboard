Fed Model — earnings yield vs bond yield

Are equities cheap or expensive relative to bonds? A rate-adjusted valuation lens.

What is the Fed Model? It compares the earnings yield of equities (= 1 ÷ P/E, expressed as a %) to the 10-year government bond yield. The gap is the Equity Risk Premium (ERP). A positive ERP means equities offer extra return over bonds. A negative ERP — like the US shows today — means government bonds offer comparable or better return for significantly less risk.

Methodology & sources

How the numbers are calculated, what the data means, and where it comes from.

What is a P/E ratio?

The Price-to-Earnings ratio divides a market's total capitalisation by its total earnings. A P/E of 25× means investors pay $25 for every $1 of annual earnings. All P/Es here are trailing twelve-month (TTM) — based on the last 12 months of actual reported earnings, not analyst forecasts. TTM is more conservative than forward P/E.

How valuation zones are set

Zones are based on historical standard deviation bands over each index's full data history:

  • Cheap / Undervalued: P/E below mean − 1 standard deviation
  • Fair value: P/E within ±1 SD of the long-run mean
  • Elevated / Caution: P/E between mean + 1SD and mean + 2SD
  • Expensive / Danger: P/E above mean + 2SD

The Nifty 50 zones follow nifty-pe-ratio.com's published framework built on 25+ years of NSE data. Limitation: A higher P/E is mathematically justifiable when interest rates are near zero. The Fed Model page accounts for this context.

Fed Model & Equity Risk Premium

The Fed Model compares the earnings yield (1 ÷ P/E, as a %) to the 10-year government bond yield. The Equity Risk Premium (ERP) = Earnings Yield − Bond Yield.

  • Positive ERP → equities offer more return than bonds — historically a constructive signal
  • Negative ERP → bonds offer comparable or better yield for lower risk — a caution signal
  • Historical US average ERP: approximately +2% to +3%

Today: CSPX earnings yield ≈ 3.58%, US 10Y ≈ 4.39% → ERP ≈ −0.81%. Nifty earnings yield ≈ 4.94%, India 10Y ≈ 6.76% → ERP ≈ −1.82%.

Caveat: The Fed Model is contested in academic literature. It conflates nominal bond yields with real earnings yields, which can be misleading in high-inflation periods.

Nifty 50 — standalone vs consolidated earnings

NSE India changed its earnings basis in April 2021 from standalone (parent company only) to consolidated (including subsidiary profits). Consolidated earnings are ~20–25% higher, so the P/E ratio fell from ~40× to ~32× overnight — not because the market got cheaper. Pre-2021 data in this dashboard uses the original standalone figures as published by NSE.
